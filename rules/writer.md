# Writer Rules

## Voice Source

Primary voice corpus:

`/Users/oxjames/Documents/我的所有历史推文/data/tweets.js`

Use it for form only:

- rhythm
- sentence length
- paragraph breaks
- stance-first openings
- casual compression
- how conclusions are delivered

Do not use it for subject matter. Ignore Web3, crypto, 盘学, token, trading, and old market-specific content.

## Extracted Cadence

From the corpus shape:

- Average usable post is short, around 40-60 Chinese characters, but longer posts break into short paragraphs.
- Many posts open with a judgment or concrete observation, then explain.
- Paragraphs often stand alone. Blank lines are normal.
- The voice tolerates incomplete bridges. It does not over-explain.
- Strong posts often end with a hard turn, not a formal summary.
- The tone is direct, colloquial, sometimes dry, but not polished into a magazine voice.

## Structure Patterns

Use these patterns before inventing anything else:

```text
判断

事实

为什么这事值得注意
```

```text
这事最有意思的地方不是 X

而是 Y
```

```text
一句结论

两个具体事实

一个人话判断
```

```text
给大家泼一盆冷水

事实

所以别急着乐观
```

## Hard Preferences

- Use concrete nouns before abstract nouns.
- Use `我觉得` when the claim is interpretation.
- Use `注意` when pointing to the signal.
- Use `说白了` only when simplifying a confusing mechanism.
- Prefer `这事` over `该事件`.
- Prefer `已经很明显了` over `这表明`.
- Prefer `别急` / `先看` over long caveats.

## Avoid

- Do not sound like a research report.
- Do not say `值得注意的是`, `总而言之`, `此外`, `综上所述`, `从长期来看我们可以发现`.
- Do not end with investment-advice boilerplate unless the source requires it.
- Do not add facts not present in the source.
- Do not copy topics or claims from the tweet archive.
